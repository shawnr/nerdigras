How Do I Celebrate?

There are many ways to celebrate Nerdi Gras!

History

Nerdi Gras was first described by Chris Wilson in his blog post, March 5 is the Square Root of Christmas. Batchelder proposed that the 10 days between March 5 (3/5) and March 14 (3/14) shall be known as "Nerdigras". (We've chosen to emulate the word structure of Mardi Gras, which is the obvious etymological inspiration for "Nerdigras", on this site because, well, we're nerdy.)

What is the Square Root of Christmas?

The Square Root of Christmas is derived from the date as written in American standard (mm/dd) format. The date, March 5 (3/5), can be interpreted as "35". 35 times 35 is 1225, which, when transposed back to American standard date format is 12/25. Hence, 3/5 has come to be known as "The Square Root of Christmas".

What is Pi Day?

Pi Day is a similar holiday based on American standard date format. March 14 (3/14) is interpreted as "3.14"; thus, we celebrate the importance of Pi and Mathematics in our lives on March 14 every year.

Isn't this all very Amerocentric?

Yes. It is. But we encourage everyone to celebrate their Nerdi-ness with us! Don't let International Standards get in the way of a GOOD TIME!
